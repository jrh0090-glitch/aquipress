Bajo la Piel is a bilingual YA novella series set in a world where vampires are registered by the government, synthetic blood is a public health debate, and two teenagers in Huntsville, Alabama are becoming impossible to ignore.

Each book follows Viviana and Marques in their own voices, in their own languages, navigating a world that wasn't built for them. The complications are real. So is what they're building, if they can just open their minds and let it happen.

Bajo la Piel is designed for high school newcomers and multilingual readers. The series pairs literary YA fiction with a paragraph-by-paragraph bilingual format grounded in second language acquisition research: full cognitive engagement, full story, no simplified content. These are real books. They just meet you where you are.

Bajo la Piel: Liar — Capítulo 1 (excerpt) · Viviana

La tarjeta del registro vive en mi bolsillo trasero. Me he memorizado su textura: la manera en que las esquinas se han suavizado, la manera en que el laminado ha empezado a separarse en un borde. Tres años cargando el mismo pedazo de plástico hacen eso.

The registry card lives in my back pocket. I've memorized the texture of it: the way the corners have gone soft, the way the laminate has started to separate at one edge. Three years carrying the same piece of plastic will do that.

Marques dice que debería mostrarlo más. Él carga el suyo en un collar. Le dije que eso es el tipo de cosa que hace que te sigan a casa, y él dijo que yo no tenía sentido de la aventura.

Marques says I should display it more. He keeps his on a lanyard. I told him that's the kind of thing that gets you followed home, and he said I had no sense of adventure.

The Research La investigación

These books are built to work.

When you read in one language, your brain doesn't turn the other one off. Bilingual readers use both languages at the same time: the words you know in Spanish help unlock the words you're learning in English, and the other way around too.

That's not just intuition. It's been documented across decades of research into how multilingual minds actually process text. Aquí Press books are designed around that reality.

The paragraph-by-paragraph bilingual format isn't a workaround — it's the point. Every structural choice in the way these books are written is grounded in the science of how people actually become readers in two languages.

🧩

El español no es un apoyo. Es la base.

The Spanish isn't there as a crutch. For a Spanish-dominant reader, it's the cognitive scaffold that makes the English accessible — removing it would make reading harder, not more "rigorous." (Cummins, 1979–2000)

⚖️

Neither language should feel secondary.

When one version of a bilingual text is a translation, it always carries less weight. Aquí Press titles are drafted in parallel: both versions are the book, written independently in each language.

🛋️

Diseñado para leer solo, en casa.

A core design goal: a student should be able to pick up this book on a couch, with no teacher nearby, and navigate the story independently. The bilingual format makes that possible. English-only does not.

🌱

Bilingual programs outperform monolingual ones.

Spanish-speaking students in bilingual programs consistently outperform peers in monolingual programs in both reading and content learning. (Marian et al., 2013)
